Different age ranges are often labeled with generational names based on shared experiences, cultural shifts, and technological advancements that shape their worldview. Here are a few examples:

Event Start End Category Summary Citations
Silent Generation 1928 1945 This generation came of age during a period of economic hardship and post-war recovery, and they are often characterized by a strong work ethic.
Baby Boomers 1946 1964 This generation experienced a significant increase in birth rates after World War II. They witnessed social and cultural changes, including the civil rights movement and the rise of television.
Generation X 1965 1980 Often referred to as the "Latchkey Generation," they were the first to grow up with widespread divorce rates and both parents working. They also witnessed the advent of personal computers and the internet.
Millennials 1981 1996 Also known as Generation Y, millennials grew up with rapid technological advancements, including the internet and mobile phones. They are often associated with a desire for work-life balance and social activism.
Generation Z 1997 2012 This generation is characterized by being true digital natives, having grown up with smartphones, social media, and instant access to information. They are often seen as diverse, entrepreneurial, and socially conscious.

It's important to note that these generational boundaries are somewhat arbitrary, and there can be variations in definitions. The terms are used to broadly describe shared experiences and characteristics within certain age groups.
