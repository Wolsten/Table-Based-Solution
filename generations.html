Different age ranges are often labeled with generational names based on shared experiences, cultural shifts, and technological advancements that shape their worldview. Here are a few examples provided by querying Chat-CGP (v3), with my commentary added:

Event Start End Category Summary Citations
Silent Generation 1928 1945

This generation came of age during a period of economic hardship and post-war recovery. According to Chat-GPT:

they are often characterized by a strong work ethic

However, having a strong work ethic misinterprets cause and effect. The AI model is correct that this generation was born in a time of economic hardship, but had no choice but to work hard because there was little in the way of welfare to fall back on. For later generations the model makes no reference to work ethic, even though many social commentators argue that some people are lazier because of easy access to welfare and widespread use of technology and automation.

Baby Boomers 1946 1964 This generation experienced a significant increase in birth rates after World War II. They witnessed social and cultural changes, including the civil rights movement and the rise of television.
Generation X 1965 1980 Often referred to as the "Latchkey Generation," they were the first to grow up with widespread divorce rates and both parents working. They also witnessed the advent of personal computers and the internet.
Millennials 1981 1996 Also known as Generation Y, millennials grew up with rapid technological advancements, including the internet and mobile phones. They are often associated with a desire for work-life balance and social activism.
Generation Z 1997 2012

This generation is characterized by being true digital natives, having grown up with smartphones, social media, and instant access to information.

According to ChatGPT:

they are often seen as diverse, entrepreneurial, and socially conscious

but this is a value judgement, presumably introduced by being trained on gen-z weighted data and trained by gen-z programmers. There is also evidence of this in the definition for millienials but not so for previous generations. i.e. feelings become more important for more recent generations.

However, If you look back to the activism of previous generations, such as those who wanted to ban the bomb then I suggest that no particular generation has any better claim on being more moral than a previous one. The AI definition is therefore somewhat narcissistic.

As Chat-GPT cautions, it's important to note that these generational boundaries are somewhat arbitrary, and there can be variations in definitions. The terms are used to broadly describe shared experiences and characteristics within certain age groups.
